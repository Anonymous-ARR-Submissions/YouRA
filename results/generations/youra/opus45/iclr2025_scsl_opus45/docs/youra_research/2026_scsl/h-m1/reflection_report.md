## Reflection Analysis

### What Succeeded
- Experiment infrastructure (extending H-E1) worked correctly
- All 5 seeds completed successfully
- 100% of samples (4,795) had curvature analyzed
- Code implementation was validated without errors

### What Failed
- **Timing Gap:** Target >= 3 epochs, Achieved: 0.20 +/- 0.40 epochs
- **Pass Rate:** Target >= 70%, Achieved: 0% (0/5 seeds)
- Both minority and majority groups stabilized at epoch 2-3

### Root Cause Analysis

1. **Fast Convergence:** Pretrained ResNet-50 converges very quickly on Waterbirds (4,795 samples). By epoch 2-3, the loss landscape has already stabilized for most samples.

2. **Curvature Sensitivity:** The curvature threshold (-0.002) and consecutive epochs (2) may be too lenient, causing both groups to "stabilize" at nearly the same early epoch.

3. **Feature vs. Mechanism:** H-E1 showed trajectory features (especially L1) predict minority membership with AUROC=0.9452. However, this discriminative signal comes from loss magnitude, not temporal curvature dynamics.

### Key Insights

1. **Magnitude > Timing:** Minority samples are distinguished by *how much* they lose (higher initial loss), not *when* their curvature stabilizes.

2. **Pretrained Model Effect:** ImageNet pretraining causes rapid convergence that may mask temporal dynamics.

3. **Alternative Signatures:** First-derivative (slope) or variance-based features may capture temporal dynamics better than second-derivative curvature.
