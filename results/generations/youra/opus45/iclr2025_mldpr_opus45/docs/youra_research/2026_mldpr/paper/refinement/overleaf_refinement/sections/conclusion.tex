
This work introduced a two-level hierarchical methodology for time series trajectory analysis combining PELT changepoint detection with DTW-based clustering. Experiments on 500 time series validated the methodology:

\begin{enumerate}
\item DTW clustering achieved silhouette score 0.352 and bootstrap Jaccard stability 0.82.
\item PELT changepoint detection identified phase transitions in 81\% of trajectories.
\item Three of four shape descriptors (growth ratio, changepoint count, derivative variance) differentiated clusters.
\item The empirical four-cluster structure mapped to two dominant behavioral patterns (slow\_burn and revival), fewer than the five hypothesized archetypes.
\end{enumerate}

The methodology is validated for time series trajectory analysis. Domain-specific applications (such as dataset adoption dynamics) require validation with appropriate domain data when available.

\subsubsection{Future Work}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Domain validation:} Apply the methodology to actual download trajectory data when API support becomes available.
\item \textbf{Archetype refinement:} Develop a data-driven archetype taxonomy based on the observed two-pattern structure.
\item \textbf{Cross-domain analysis:} Evaluate whether the two-pattern structure generalizes across different time series domains.
\end{enumerate}
