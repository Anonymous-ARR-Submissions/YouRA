\subsubsection{Baseline Performance}

The baseline model converged to validation perplexity 59.34 after 5000 steps. Training loss decreased monotonically from ~10 to 4.82. This confirms the experimental setup (dataset, architecture, optimizer, hyperparameters) produces functional language models.

\subsubsection{Regularized Model Performance}

The regularized model exhibited three pathologies:

\textbf{1. Zero Stable Rank Measurements}: All 12 layers returned exactly 0.0 for stable rank at every checkpoint. Per-layer measurements:
\begin{itemize}
\item Layer 0-11: 0.0 (all layers)
\item Mean: 0.0
\item Standard deviation: 0.0
\end{itemize}

\textbf{2. Perplexity Explosion}: Validation perplexity increased from 59.34 (baseline) to 45,792.62 (regularized), representing 77,065\% deviation. Trajectory analysis shows divergence beginning around step 1500, with acceleration after step 3000.

\textbf{3. Negative Regularization Losses}: At step 4500:
\begin{itemize}
\item CLM loss: 10.76
\item Regularization loss: -17,529,929,728
\item Total loss: -116,297,128
\item Lambda: 0.0063 (decayed from 0.01)
\end{itemize}

The regularization term magnitude exceeded CLM loss by nine orders of magnitude despite adaptive tuning.
