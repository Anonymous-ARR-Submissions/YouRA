
Three research questions are addressed:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{RQ1 (H-E1):} Do cross-property partial Spearman correlations exceed |ρ| ≥ 0.40 with BCa CIs excluding zero, and does factor analysis extract a stable latent dimension?
\item \textbf{RQ2 (H-M1):} Does the calibration–hallucination correlation survive MMLU capability control?
\item \textbf{RQ3 (H-M2):} Does the epistemic composite predict top-quartile AdvGLUE failure with ΔAUC ≥ 0.10 over MMLU-only?
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{4.1 Benchmarks}

All metrics are synthetically generated to conform to lm-evaluation-harness v0.4.x conventions under greedy decoding (temperature = 0). T=0.7 stochastic replication is pre-registered for real-data execution (FW1) but was not executed in this study.

\subsubsection{4.2 Baselines}

The MMLU-only LOO logistic classifier serves as the capability-only screening baseline. The ΔAUC comparison quantifies the marginal value of epistemic reliability metrics over capability screening alone.

\subsubsection{4.3 Implementation Details}

\begin{itemize}
\item Partial Spearman correlation: scipy.stats.spearmanr
\item BCa bootstrap: B=10,000, numpy seed 42
\item Factor analysis: factor_analyzer v0.4.x, Kaiser normalization
\item LOO cross-validation: scikit-learn LeaveOneOut, LogisticRegression (C=1.0, max_iter=1000)
\item Per-fold StandardScaler (no data leakage across folds)
\item ΔAUC: paired bootstrap, B=10,000
\end{itemize}
