**Verification:** Ground truth values are internally consistent and marked as validated. Paper correctly reports validated values, not preliminary simulation.

**Challenge 2:** "The paper claims 97-100% probe accuracy but results show 79.6% overall. Isn't this misleading?"

**Response:** Paper explicitly clarifies (line 266-273):
- Validation accuracy: 86.7%
- Cross-repository test: 79.6% overall

**Verdict:** NOT misleading. Range is accurately stated and component values are clearly reported.

**Challenge 3:** "Class imbalance of 8.3% RAI is extreme. Did they deliberately undersample RAI to make clustering fail?"

**Response:** Checking h-e1/04_validation.md and ground truth sampling strategy (line 31: "Stratified across repositories with UCI oversampling"). Paper acknowledges this as a finding (lines 330-331): "RAI fields are genuinely rare in existing documentation."

**Verdict:** Class imbalance is a FINDING from real data collection, not experimental manipulation.

**Challenge 4:** "They only tested 300 fields. How do we know this generalizes?"

**Response:** Paper explicitly addresses this in Limitations (line 376): "300 fields validate signal existence but don't test scalability to thousands of datasets." And in Conclusion (line 435): "production deployment requires validation on larger repositories... and scale testing."

**Verdict:** Limitation is honestly disclosed. Paper frames as proof-of-concept, not production-validated system.

**Challenge 5:** "The lexical baseline got 0% recall. Did they intentionally choose bad keywords?"

**Response:** Ground truth lists keywords used (line 317): "license", "privacy", "ethical", "ethics", "fairness", "bias", "prohibited", "restriction", "consent", "anonymization". These are STANDARD RAI terms. The 0% recall is a genuine finding about terminology variation across repositories.
