## Section Summaries

### Abstract
The rapid integration of generative AI into everyday life underscores the need to move beyond unidirectional alignment models that only adapt AI to human values. This workshop focuses on bidirectional human-AI alignment, a dynamic, reciprocal process where humans and AI co-adapt through interaction, evaluation, and value-centered design. Building on past workshops, it aims to advance value-centered AI and human-AI collaboration by embedding human and societal values into alignment research via interdisciplinary discussions and collaborative activities.

### Introduction & Motivation
The rise of generative AI necessitates aligning these technologies with human values and societal principles to prevent negative consequences while maximizing societal benefits. Traditional AI alignment approaches have been unidirectional—focused solely on shaping AI systems to meet predefined human outcomes. However, the paper argues for a shift toward bidirectional alignment where both AI and humans adapt to each other over time, thus fostering a more dynamic interaction that acknowledges evolving relationships and ongoing human engagement with AI.
