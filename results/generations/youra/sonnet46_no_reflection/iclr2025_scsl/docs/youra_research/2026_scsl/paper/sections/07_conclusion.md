# 7. Conclusion

We began with a stark observation: the same k-means clustering pipeline — identical model, identical hyperparameters, identical code — achieves an AMI of 0.762 on Waterbirds and 0.258 on CelebA. Both datasets are standard benchmarks for spurious correlation robustification; both degrade worst-group accuracy under ERM; both are routinely used to validate annotation-free detection methods. Yet the detection mechanism activates in one case and fails categorically in the other.

The explanation is not in the algorithm but in the pretraining. ImageNet-pretrained ResNet-50 already encodes bird habitats as separable semantic dimensions before any task-specific training; it does not encode hair color. When fine-tuned with ERM, the model immediately surfaces the pretrained background representation as cluster-separable structure in Waterbirds embeddings. CelebA offers no such pretrained prior to activate, and five epochs of ERM fine-tuning are insufficient to build hair-color separable structure from scratch.

This finding — which we term *feature-strength conditionality* — is a previously undocumented constraint on all clustering-based annotation-free spurious detection methods. Methods that cluster representation embeddings to discover spurious groups (GEORGE, PruSC, and their derivatives) implicitly assume that spurious features are cluster-discoverable at the probe epoch and with the chosen k. Our results show that this assumption holds for scene-level spurious attributes but fails for fine-grained texture-based attributes, and that the critical factor is the alignment of the spurious attribute with the ImageNet pretraining prior.

**Summary of contributions.** We characterize feature-strength conditionality through a controlled experiment (C1), quantify the gap empirically with two complementary metrics and random baselines (C2), propose a pretrained linear probe pre-screen as a practical diagnostic (C3), and identify configuration-sensitivity as a gap in how published clustering-based detection results are reported (C4).

**Future directions.** Three results-grounded directions follow directly from our findings. First, replacing the fixed epoch-5 probe with an adaptive probe epoch based on representational stability (FW1) would address the non-universality of epoch 5 across datasets of different sizes. Second, using GMM with BIC-guided k selection (FW2) would address the underspecification of k=2 for datasets with multi-group spurious structure such as CelebA. Third, validating the downstream Gradient SNR Balancing intervention on Waterbirds — where the detection step is confirmed reliable — would provide mechanistic evidence for the gradient SNR hypothesis even without a fully general detection step (FW3). Each of these directions is motivated by a specific experimental finding rather than a generic call for more experiments.

Before asking whether annotation-free robustification works, practitioners should ask a prior question: does the pretrained model already know the spurious attribute? The answer to that question determines whether clustering-based detection is trustworthy — and whether any downstream intervention is operating on meaningful spurious directions or on noise.
